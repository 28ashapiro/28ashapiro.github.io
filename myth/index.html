Prometheus and Man
Explanations - Web Source - Rough Draft
Summary
--Prometheus creates Man, with Athena--
Two titans, Prometheus and Epimetheus, were spared imprisonment in Tartarus after the war between the Titans and Olympians since they had remained neutral. Instead they were ordered to make humans. First, Prometheus sculpted them out of clay, Athena breathing life into the figures.
--Epimetheus and Man--
Epimetheus was assigned to give the creatures of Earth their qualities. The animals are swift, cunning, strong, having fur, and the ability to fly via wings. When he got to man, Epimetheus had run out of good qualities, so there were none given to man. But Prometheus decided to make man stand upright–just like the gods–and gave them fire.
--Prometheus' Deceit and Trickery--
Prometheus loved man more than the Olympians, who had banished most of his family to Tartarus. So when Zeus ordered that man must sacrifice a portion of each food to the gods, Prometheus planned a little trick. He made two piles; one bones wrapped in fat, and the other meat hidden in hide. Zeus, unaware of the trick, chose the bones as his share. Since he had given his word, Zeus was forced to accept the bones as his share for future sacrifices. Zeus, enraged, took fire away from man. However, Prometheus lit a torch from the sun and brought it to man. Zeus, enraged again, decided to inflict a punishment on both man and Prometheus.
--Zeus' Punishment for Mankind--
To punish man, Zeus ordered Haphaestus to create a mortal of stunning beauty. The gods gave this mortal many gifts of wealth, and Hermes gave it a deceptive heart and a lying tongue. The final gift, a jar of which they were forbidden to open. Thus, Zeus sent the mortal–Pandora–to live amongst men.
--Epimetheus' and Pandora's Mistake--
Prometheus had warned his brother, Epimetheus, not to accept any gifts from Zeus. But even then, Pandora’s beauty was too great, so he let her stay. Her curiosity about the jar overwhelmed her, Pandora opened it, and so she released evils amongst the world. Though the jar was not empty, hope was left in the jar, being unseen by Pandora as she sealed the jar once more.
--Prometheus Captured--
Zeus was angry at Prometheus for three reasons, the trick, returning fire to man, and refusing to tell Zeus which of his kids would overthrow him. So, Zeus commanded his servants Force and Violence–yes that’s their real names–to seize Prometheus, take him to the Caucasus Mountains and chain him to a rock with unbreakable, diamond chains.
--Promtheus is Freed--
In the Caucasus Mountains Prometheus lay, there he was tormented day and night by a giant eagle, tearing at his liver. Zeus gave him two ways out of his torment, telling Zeus who is the mother of the child that will dethrone him, or to meet two conditions. These conditions state that an immortal must sacrifice himself for Prometheus, and a mortal must kill the eagle and unchain him. Eventually, Chiron the Centaur agreed to being the immortal sacrifice, and Heracles killed the eagle and unbound him from his torment.
